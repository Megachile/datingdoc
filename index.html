Intro:

Headline:I'm Adam, a 34 year old PhD student at the University of New Mexico in Albuquerque looking for the love of my life and mother of my kids.

Relationship goal:I've been happily single for a long time and I'll continue to be happy on my own as long as I need to be. In the past few years I've opened my eyes to the aspiration to become a lot more than happy: to find someone very special to invest in creating a deeply loving, supportive, transformative relationship that can be the foundation to build a family.

Practicality: I live in Albuquerque and will be here until I finish my PhD, hopefully in spring 2028. I realize this is a significant obstacle, and it makes this gesture a bit of a long shot for the time being. But the relationship I'm looking for isn't one of convenience or happenstance. I want to find someone I can choose to commit to for the long term, to practice overcoming challenges with. If there's a chance I might meet that person a few years earlier, I can't put it off even if it isn't easy.

Character traits I have to offer:

Curious: I love to learn about people and about the universe, and I care about getting things right.

Grateful: I have been blessed with a wonderful family, befriended many incredible people, and had amazing opportunities, but the biggest blessing is that I've had the right attitude to properly value my good fortune.

Friendly Ambitious Nerd: I founded Gallformers.org, built connections between amateur naturalists and academia, and personally built a web tool and a browser extension.

Socially competent: I generally like small talk. I’m a teacher and tutor who can communicate complex ideas well—sometimes. My dance partners seem to quickly feel comfortable with me.

Introspective: Perhaps prone to overthinking and overcommunicating, but when it’s relevant, I spend a lot of time reflecting on my feelings and relational behavior.

Affectionate: I like to let my friends know I like them 🙂

Sharp: I practice attuned, attentive teasing. I hold myself and others to high standards, but relish our many slapstick failures to live up to them.

Exuberant: I love to find beauty in the world—especially the big, old, strange, and grotesque aspects. I love human culture as the highest expression of the diversity and intricacy of the natural world.
